FAQ

Penetration testing, also known as penetest, is a method of testing the security of your IT infrastructure by simulating hacker attacks. The goal of penetest is to identify vulnerabilities in systems that can be exploited by attackers to gain unauthorized access to data or disrupt systems.

Penetest simulates real-world cyberattacks, testing the possibility of unauthorized access or penetration of systems, while a security audit is a broader process of analyzing information security policies and processes. Penetest focuses on vulnerability detection, while auditing covers more global aspects of security.

Yes, penetest is performed under controlled conditions by professionals to avoid disruption to your systems. We carefully plan each step and coordinate it with your team to minimize possible risks and prevent damage.

It is recommended to perform penetration testing at least once a year, as well as when there are significant changes in the infrastructure (for example, when implementing new systems or applications). Regular testing allows you to identify and remediate vulnerabilities in a timely manner.
